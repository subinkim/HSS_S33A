My Hometown

  • As I have briefly mentioned in the home page of the website, I am from South Korea, which is part of East Asia, between Japan and China. Among hundreds of cities in Korea, I was born and raised on an island called "Jeju", which is the island on the south of the peninsula.
  • The island retains the original beauty of its nature, including the blue, shining sea that encompasses the island , and Mt. Halla that stands audaciously at the heart of Jeju. It is also known for the small mountains which were created when Mt. Halla, which was an active volcano in the past, erupted.
  • 0.7 million people reside on this island with 1850km2 of area, including 20 000 people of foreign nationality.
  • The main industries on the island are agriculture and tourism. Hence, it is a boring place for young people like me, who prefer visiting theme parks than walking through the forests.
  • The island carries huge values in science. Due to its nature of being a volcanic island, its topographical features are an important source of study. The island itself with its lava tubes is UNESCO world heritage site, and the UNESCO describes them as follows: "The site, of outstanding aesthetic beauty, also bears testimony to the history of the planet, its features and processes."
